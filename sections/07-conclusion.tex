\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conc}

% Summarize: RCT, Affinity Tensor, U_RA gate.
% State what has been proven vs. proposed.
% Point to companion papers.

We have introduced the Relational Coherence Theorem, a formal framework
that formalizes the relational structure implied by Bell's theorem and
relational quantum mechanics~\cite{Bell1964,Rovelli1996}.
The \RCT~comprises four propositions characterizing the conditions under which
quantum properties emerge, are sustained, and are lost through environmental
decoherence.

The Affinity Tensor~$\alpha(S,E)$ provides an operationalized, computable
measure of relational coherence, related to but distinct from concurrence
and von Neumann entropy.
The Relational Affinity Gate~$\URA(\alpha)$ is derived from the \RCT~as
a continuously parameterized two-qubit gate optimized for coherence preservation.
Numerical simulations demonstrate measurable fidelity advantages in
high-decoherence regimes.

The philosophical implications of the \RCT~--- including the argument
that process ontology is the only internally consistent metaphysical
framework for quantum mechanics~--- are developed in the companion paper.
The Bahá'í metaphysical correspondence is examined in a third companion paper.
