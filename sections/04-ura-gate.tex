\section{The Relational Affinity Gate}
\label{sec:gate}

\subsection{Definition}

The \emph{Relational Affinity Gate} is defined as the two-qubit unitary
\begin{equation}
  \URA(\alpha) = \exp\!\left(-\frac{i\alpha}{2}
    \bigl(\sigma_x \otimes \sigma_x
         + \sigma_y \otimes \sigma_y
         + \sigma_z \otimes \sigma_z\bigr)\right),
  \label{eq:URA}
\end{equation}
where $\alpha \in [0, \pi/2]$ is the Affinity Tensor parameter and
$\sigma_{x,y,z}$ are the Pauli matrices.

\subsection{Bell State Production}

At $\alpha = \pi/4$:
\begin{equation}
  \URA(\pi/4)\ket{00}
    = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\bigl(\ket{00} + \ket{11}\bigr)
    = \ket{\Phi^+},
\end{equation}
the maximally entangled Bell state, corresponding to $\bar\alpha = 1$
(maximal relational affinity).

\subsection{Weyl Chamber Analysis}

The local equivalence class of~$\URA$ in the Weyl chamber of
$\mathrm{SU}(4)/[\mathrm{SU}(2)\otimes\mathrm{SU}(2)]$~\cite{Zhang2003WeylChamber}
is characterized by coordinates $(c_1, c_2, c_3)$ that depend continuously on~$\alpha$.
At $\alpha = 0$: $\URA = \mathbf{I}$ (identity, no entanglement).
At $\alpha = \pi/4$: $\URA$ reaches maximum entanglement capability.

\subsection{Entanglement Capability}

% TODO: compute C(alpha) = entanglement capability as function of alpha.
% Compare: CNOT, iSWAP, CZ are fixed points; U_RA continuously parameterized.

The entanglement capability $\mathcal{C}(\alpha)$ of~$\URA$, measured
by the maximum concurrence achievable over all input states, varies
continuously with~$\alpha$.
This continuous parameterization~---~absent from fixed gates such as
\textsc{cnot}, \textsc{iswap}, and \textsc{cz}~---~allows the gate
to be tuned to the coherence regime of the physical device.
