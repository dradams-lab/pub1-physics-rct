Bell's theorem~\cite{Bell1964} and its loophole-free experimental
verification~\cite{Aspect1982,Zeilinger2022Nobel} rule out local
non-contextual hidden-variable completions of quantum mechanics.
A natural response, on which this work builds, is the relational
reading of Rovelli~\cite{Rovelli1996,Rovelli2021}, on which quantum
properties are not assigned to systems in isolation but are encoded
in their interaction histories.
We package one technically explicit aspect of this reading into the
\emph{Relational Coherence framework} (\RCT), centred on the
\emph{Affinity Tensor}~$\alpha(S,E)$~---~the off-diagonal part of the
reduced state $\rho_S$ in the einselected pointer basis~---~whose
normalized Frobenius norm $\bar\alpha(S,E) \in [0,1]$ is the
Hilbert--Schmidt coherence quantifier of $\rho_S$ in that basis.
We collect four standard identities (property indefiniteness,
exponential decay under Markovian dephasing, range and extremal
characterization, and coherence redistribution under unitary $S$--$E$
evolution) in the language of~$\bar\alpha$, suitable for use as a
per-layer coherence-budget diagnostic in two-qubit circuits.

Building on existing parametric-gate
work~\cite{LossDiVincenzo1998,Foxen2020,Abrams2020,Lacroix2020,Caldwell2018},
we examine the isotropic-Heisenberg-exchange unitary
$\URA(\theta) = \exp[-i\theta(\sigma_x{\otimes}\sigma_x +
\sigma_y{\otimes}\sigma_y + \sigma_z{\otimes}\sigma_z)/2]$ with
$\theta \in [0,\pi/2]$, which traces the diagonal $(\theta,\theta,\theta)$
of the SU(4) Weyl chamber~\cite{Zhang2003WeylChamber} and attains
maximum entanglement capability at $\theta = \pi/4$ ($\sqrt{\textsc{swap}}$).
On platforms where the Heisenberg-exchange pulse duration scales
linearly with~$\theta$, $\URA(\pi/8)$ accumulates half the
coherence-limited error of a full $\theta = \pi/4$ entangler.
Numerical simulations in Qiskit~\cite{Qiskit2024} and Cirq~\cite{Cirq2025}
under gate-time-proportional depolarizing, dephasing, and amplitude-damping
noise yield a per-gate fidelity advantage of
$\Delta F \in [+0.038, +0.039]$ at $p_{\rm base} = 0.10$ over
\textsc{cnot}, \textsc{iswap}, and \textsc{cz}, consistent with the
experimental continuous-fSim advantage reported by Foxen~\textit{et~al.}\
on Sycamore~\cite{Foxen2020}.
The advantage is contingent on direct exchange calibration; on platforms
where $\URA(\theta)$ must be decomposed into fixed native primitives, the
advantage may shrink or vanish.
